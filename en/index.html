About

Real story, not a resume template

I came from a humanities track in high school and studied Chinese language and literature in college. During those years, I kept searching for truly useful materials, but most of what I found was repetitive and copy-based.

So I started building my own system: literary theory, foreign literature history, modern Chinese literature, classical literature, and linguistics. After graduation, I realized literature and film alone were not enough for the questions I cared about, so I moved deeper into philosophy and psychology.

What I’m trying to build is simple: better sources and better learning routes, with lower barriers. I care less about speed, and more about whether what I build is genuinely useful to real learners.
